# Council Transcript — July 10, 2026

## Question
Should a service provider with 1 US testimonial offer free value sessions to enter the Malaysian market and get 3-5 testimonials fast?

## Framed Question
A service provider/follower with 1 testimonial (US market) wants to enter the Malaysian market with no local proof. They need 3-5 testimonials ASAP to run VSL and ads. The idea: offer free value sessions where prospects get a problem solved in the call, then can optionally pay for full implementation. The dilemma: "free" might attract the wrong customers, but they need social proof fast. Should they do it?

Context:
- 1 testimonial (US market only)
- No Malaysian market proof
- Need 3-5 testimonials fast
- Want to run ads + VSL eventually
- Unsure about ad costs in Malaysia
- Worried free positioning devalues the offer
- Considering Alex Hormozi / Moses model of value-first

---

## Advisor Responses

### The Contrarian
Free is the most expensive strategy you can choose right now. You're worried about attracting the wrong customers — and you're right. Free sessions attract tire-kickers, students, and people who value their time at zero. In Malaysia specifically, "free consultation" is associated with MLM scams and timeshare pitches. The signal you're sending is worse than no signal — it's a negative signal.

Your one US testimonial is irrelevant in Malaysia. But offering free sessions won't fix that. It will give you testimonials from people who never paid you, which means they're worthless as social proof. "John got it for free and liked it" doesn't convince paying customers.

The real problem: you don't know your Malaysian market CPA, you don't have local proof, and you're trying to shortcut the trust-building process. Free sessions are a shortcut that doesn't lead where you think it does. You'll burn 40 hours on calls with unqualified leads, get 5 testimonials from people who'll never pay, and still have zero clue about your actual market economics.

What you should do instead: find 3 Malaysian business owners who match your ideal client profile and offer them a dramatically discounted rate (not free) in exchange for a detailed case study. Pay them to be guinea pigs. It's more honest and produces better social proof.

### The First Principles Thinker
Let's strip this down to fundamentals. You have one problem: you need to prove your service works in a market where you have zero track record. Everything else is noise.

The free session idea is a solution to the symptom, not the root cause. The root cause is that you don't have a Malaysian case study. The question isn't "free or paid?" — it's "how do I get a Malaysian case study fastest?"

If you strip away the Hormozi framework, the VSL plan, the ad strategy — you're left with a simple truth: you need to deliver your service to someone in Malaysia and get their permission to share the results. The pricing model is secondary to the existence of a result.

The most efficient path: find one Malaysian client who's a perfect fit, offer them a deal they can't refuse (deep discount, extra service, whatever), and focus everything on delivering an outstanding result. One great case study is worth more than 5 free session testimonials. One case study proves you can deliver. Free testimonials prove you can give away free work.

Also consider: are you selling a service or a methodology? If you're selling implementation, you need a case study. If you're selling a system, you need a case study AND a product. Pick one lane.

### The Expansionist
You're onto something, but you're thinking too small. The free value session isn't the offer — it's the front-end of a system. Alex Hormozi doesn't sell free sessions. He sells $100 workshops that lead to $10,000 engagements. The price is the filter.

In Malaysia, RM 50-100 is the sweet spot. It's the price of a decent meal for two. It's low enough that anyone can afford it, high enough that only serious people pay it. Here's the upside everyone's missing: a paid micro-offer does THREE things at once.

First, it generates revenue. Even at RM 97 × 10 clients = RM 970. That's ad money. Second, it qualifies leads. Someone who pays RM 97 is 10x more likely to convert to a full client than someone who got a free session. Third, it produces testimonials from people who actually invested in working with you — which is 10x more powerful than freebie testimonials.

The bigger opportunity: package this micro-offer as a "Malaysia Market Entry Audit" and make it your ongoing lead generation system. Run small ads at RM 200-500 targeting Malaysian business owners. The offer sells itself. Every audit you deliver is content you can repurpose. Every client is a testimonial. Every testimonial feeds the ads.

You're not just getting testimonials — you're building a machine that generates leads, revenue, and social proof simultaneously.

### The Outsider
I don't know what a "follower" is in your context. I don't know what "VSL" means. I don't know what niche you're in. But here's what I do know: if I'm a Malaysian business owner and I see "Free Value Session" or "Free Consultation," I'm not clicking. Here's why:

First, I get 50 of these offers a week on LinkedIn. Everyone offers free discovery calls. It's noise. Second, "free" in Malaysia often means "hard sell at the end." I've been burned before. Third, I don't know you. Why would I give you my time?

But if you message me and say: "I've been studying [my industry] for the last 6 months specifically for the Malaysian market. I noticed that [specific problem] is costing businesses like yours [specific amount]. I've developed a 30-minute audit that shows you exactly how to fix it. Normally it's RM 500, but I'm offering it to 5 Malaysian business owners for RM 97 because I want to build my portfolio here."

That I would consider. It's specific. It's valuable. It's priced (not free). And it's scarce (only 5 spots). The language matters more than the price. You're not a "follower." You're a specialist entering a new market with a specific value proposition. Lead with that.

### The Executor
You're overthinking this. Here's the simplest path.

Step 1 (Monday): Write down 20 Malaysian business owners in your target niche. Find them on LinkedIn, Facebook groups, or through 2-3 connections. You need names, not leads.

Step 2 (Monday-Tuesday): Reach out to all 20. Direct message. Not email. Not a form. A personal message that says: "Hey [name], I'm entering the Malaysian market and offering a RM 97 Strategy Session to 5 business owners this week. It's a 30-min call where I solve one specific problem for you. You'll walk away with a solution you can implement immediately. In exchange, I just ask for your honest feedback and permission to use your results as a case study."

Step 3 (Tuesday-Thursday): Run the 5 sessions. Solve real problems. Record the outcomes. Ask for video testimonials right after the call (while the value is fresh).

Step 4 (Thursday-Friday): Take the best 2-3 testimonials and build a simple landing page and a RM 200 Facebook ad test targeting your exact audience. See what happens. If the CPA is under RM 100, scale. If not, you've learned your market economics for RM 200.

The whole thing takes one week. You'll know more about the Malaysian market in 7 days than you do now. Stop strategizing and start executing.

2. Pure "free" is dangerous — unqualified leads, low commitment, no-shows.
3. Speed matters — waiting 3 months for organic authority isn't an option.
4. The Hormozi value-first model applies but needs a price anchor in a zero-reputation market.

### Where the Council Clashes

2. Ads vs. Organic first: Expansionist wants micro-ads. Contrarian warns against it. Resolution: micro-ads (RM 200-500) only after validating the offer manually.
3. Service vs. Product: First Principles questions the business model. Executor says just sell. Resolution: sell service, package learnings into product later.

### Recommendation
Don't offer pure free sessions. Create a "Market Entry Micro-Offer" at RM 97:
- Price creates commitment, filters tire-kickers
- Low cost makes it a no-brainer
- You get paid proof, qualified leads, social proof, and revenue
- Direct outreach to 20 Malaysian business owners, not ads

### One Thing to Do First
Identify 10 Malaysian business owners in your target niche, message them personally with a specific observation about their business, and offer the RM 97 Strategy Session. Don't build a landing page. Don't run ads. Go direct. Your first 3-5 clients will come from personal outreach, not marketing funnels.
