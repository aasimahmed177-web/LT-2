LLM Council Verdict

Should you offer free value sessions to get testimonials in a new market?

Council convened: July 10, 2026

The Question

A service provider with 1 testimonial (US market) wants to enter the Malaysian market with no local proof. They need 3-5 testimonials ASAP to run VSL and ads. The idea: offer free value sessions where prospects get a problem solved, then optionally pay for full implementation. The dilemma: "free" attracts the wrong customers, but they need social proof fast.

⚖️ Chairman's Verdict

AGREEMENT The core problem is trust, not pricing. Malaysian market doesn't know you. One US testimonial is irrelevant. You need local proof.

AGREEMENT Pure "free" is dangerous. Unqualified leads, no-shows, and the "free consultation = MLM scam" association in Malaysia will kill your momentum before you start.

AGREEMENT The Hormozi value-first model applies — but it needs a price anchor to work in a market where you have zero reputation.

CLASH Free vs. Low-cost. The Contrarian says free is a trap. The Executor says free is fastest. Resolution: RM 97 micro-offer creates commitment without being a real purchase decision.

BLIND SPOT The Outsider caught: Your language ("follower", "value session", "VSL") is insider jargon. A Malaysian business owner has no idea what they're getting. Frame by outcome, not methodology.

BLIND SPOT Distribution gap: None of the advisors discussed how to get the first 3-5 people without ads. Direct outreach to specific Malaysian business owners is the answer.

⚡ Key Insight: Every advisor independently arrived at don't do free. Do a micro-offer. The price creates commitment, filters tire-kickers, and the low cost makes it a no-brainer. You get testimonials AND revenue AND qualification.

The Recommendation

Create a "Market Entry Micro-Offer" at RM 97:

  • Positioning: "Strategy Session" or "Growth Audit" — not "free consultation"
  • Structure: 30-min call, diagnose one problem, give them a done-for-you solution
  • Exchange: They get a real solution. You get a testimonial (discount session if they provide video testimonial + case study permission)
  • Distribution: Direct outreach to 20-30 Malaysian business owners. No ads needed yet.

The One Thing to Do First

Identify 10 Malaysian business owners in your target niche, message them personally with a specific, valuable observation about their business, and offer the RM 97 Strategy Session. Don't build a landing page. Don't run ads. Don't create content. Go direct.

Advisor Responses

🛑 The Contrarian — "Free is a trap" ▼

Free is the most expensive strategy you can choose right now. Free sessions attract tire-kickers, students, and people who value their time at zero. In Malaysia specifically, "free consultation" is associated with MLM scams and timeshare pitches. The signal you're sending is worse than no signal — it's a negative signal.

Your one US testimonial is irrelevant in Malaysia. But offering free sessions won't fix that. It will give you testimonials from people who never paid you, which means they're worthless as social proof. "John got it for free and liked it" doesn't convince paying customers.

What you should do instead: find 3 Malaysian business owners who match your ideal client profile and offer them a dramatically discounted rate (not free) in exchange for a detailed case study. Pay them to be guinea pigs. It's more honest and produces better social proof.

🔍 First Principles — "What are you actually solving?" ▼

You have one problem: you need to prove your service works in a market where you have zero track record. Everything else is noise.

The free session idea is a solution to the symptom, not the root cause. The question isn't "free or paid?" — it's "how do I get a Malaysian case study fastest?"

The most efficient path: find one Malaysian client who's a perfect fit, offer them a deal they can't refuse, and focus everything on delivering an outstanding result. One great case study is worth more than 5 free session testimonials.

🚀 The Expansionist — "Build a system, not a crutch" ▼

In Malaysia, RM 50-100 is the sweet spot. A paid micro-offer does THREE things at once: generates revenue (RM 97 × 10 = RM 970 for ads), qualifies leads (10x better conversion vs. free), and produces testimonials from people who actually invested.

Package this as a "Malaysia Market Entry Audit" — your ongoing lead generation system. Run small ads at RM 200-500. Every audit is repurposable content. Every client is a testimonial. Every testimonial feeds the ads.

👁️ The Outsider — "I have no idea what you're selling" ▼

If I'm a Malaysian business owner seeing "Free Value Session," I'm not clicking. I get 50 of these a week on LinkedIn. "Free" in Malaysia often means "hard sell at the end."

But if you message me: "I've been studying [my industry] for 6 months. I noticed [specific problem] is costing businesses like yours [specific amount]. I've developed a 30-minute audit to fix it. Normally RM 500, but I'm offering it to 5 business owners for RM 97 to build my portfolio." — that I'd consider. It's specific. It's priced. It's scarce. Lead with that.

⚡ The Executor — "Do this Monday morning" ▼

Step 1: List 20 Malaysian business owners in your niche.

Step 2: DM them: "RM 97 Strategy Session — 30 min, I solve one problem, you get a solution you can implement immediately."

Step 3: Run 5 sessions. Ask for video testimonials right after the call.

Step 4: Build a landing page, run RM 200 FB ad test. Learn CPA. Scale if under RM 100.

Whole thing takes one week. Stop strategizing.
